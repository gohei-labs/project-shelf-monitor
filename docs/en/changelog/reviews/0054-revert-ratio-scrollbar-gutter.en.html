Revert the ratio; stabilize the scrollbar gutter

This review asks for approval to revert the narrow-list / wide-card ratio (review 0053) back to the original layout, and to stop the page from shifting sideways depending on whether a scrollbar is present.

Requested Approval

  1. Revert the list / detail ratio from review 0053. The non-review pages (guardrail, development rules, requirements, features) go back to the original master-detail proportion: a wide list on the left + a fixed 384px detail card on the right. The flipped narrow-list / wide-card felt off to the owner. The 1440px content width (review 0052) is kept, and the list rows are restored to their fuller form (the venue, components, and priority are back in the row).
  2. Stabilize the scrollbar gutter. Add scrollbar-gutter: stable to the page root on every monitor page, so a page that has a vertical scrollbar (e.g. the guardrail monitor, many items) and a page that does not (e.g. development rules, few items) keep their centered content at the same horizontal position. Previously the content shifted left/right between pages.

Risk

Low; presentation only. scrollbar-gutter is supported in current Chrome, Edge, Firefox, and Safari 16+; older engines simply ignore it and keep the previous slight shift, so there is no hard breakage. Verified: with the gutter reserved, the centered content sits at the same left offset on a page with a scrollbar and a page without one.
